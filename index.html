Is Medicaid Really the Highest-Paying Insurance Company for Mental Health Services?

By Renee Onque | April 13, 2021

Mental health has become a major focal point for American citizens following the pandemic. According to Mental Health America, there was a 93 percent increase in people who were screened for anxiety and a 62 percent increase in people who were screened for depression since 2019.

Yet, receiving mental health support through psychotherapy is not cheap, especially if your insurance isn’t great. Private insurance companies provide different rates for therapy depending on how much coverage a person has. The more benefits you have, the more the insurance company is willing to pay.

Public assistance programs like Medicaid are insurance companies specifically for low-income U.S. citizens. Medicaid is cited as “the single largest payer for mental health services in the United States,” on their website, but is this really true?
